# Local Farm First Homepage Redesign

## Purpose

Rebuild the Garden of Weeden homepage around a Local Farm First / Farm to Flame brand story. The homepage should make the business feel rooted in Buffalo and Western NY craft cannabis, connected to local growers, and more trustworthy than a generic dispensary before it asks visitors to shop.

## Approved Direction

Garden of Weeden should lead as a local cannabis microbusiness built around Farm to Flame. The brand should feel like a warm dark garden, a premium craft cannabis destination, and a regional farm network rather than a fantasy-themed or generic ecommerce storefront.

The homepage should explicitly say Garden of Weeden carries local, small-batch cannabis from its own farm south of Buffalo and from small craft growers across Central and Western NY. It should also explicitly mention Cannabis Farmers Alliance.

As a NYS microbusiness with a farm 15 miles south of Buffalo, Farm to Flame should also mean there is no middleman on Garden of Weeden name-brand flower and pre-rolls. When a customer buys Garden of Weeden branded flower or a Garden of Weeden joint, they are buying cannabis grown by the same company they are buying it from. Most dispensaries have to buy flower from another producer, but Garden of Weeden's own branded products come from its own farm. Other products should be framed as locally sourced from regional farms and microbusinesses that Garden of Weeden knows and has strong relationships with through the Cannabis Farmers Alliance.

## Conversion Priorities

1. Build trust in the local farm and craft cannabis story.
2. Drive lounge, event, private event, and mobile weed bar booking inquiries, with copy framed around New York cannabis rule compliance.
3. Let visitors view the current menu through the All Leaves menu path without making ecommerce the main brand experience.

## Required Brand Messages

- Farm to Flame is the central brand idea.
- Garden of Weeden is a NYS licensed cannabis microbusiness with a dispensary and on-site consumption space.
- Products are sourced from Garden of Weeden's farm south of Buffalo and other small craft growers across Central and Western NY.
- Garden of Weeden name-brand flower and pre-rolls come from Garden of Weeden's own farm, with no middleman between the grower and the customer.
- Garden of Weeden supports small family farms, women-owned businesses, veteran-owned businesses, minority-owned businesses, microbusinesses, and producers connected to Cannabis Farmers Alliance.
- Other carried products come from local and regional farms, microbusinesses, and producers with direct relationships through Cannabis Farmers Alliance.
- Budtenders know the farmer partners, cultivation practices, and product stories behind what is on the shelf.
- The Forbidden Fruit lounge is part of the experience and can support events, private events, and bookable mobile weed bar inquiries planned within New York cannabis rules.
- Cycling should appear only as a subtle local convenience signal, not as a major brand theme.

## Homepage Structure

### 1. Hero: Farm to Flame Cannabis

The hero should lead with the brand and the local craft promise.

Primary headline direction:

> Garden of Weeden

Supporting headline direction:

> Farm to Flame Cannabis

Supporting copy should explain that Garden of Weeden offers local, small-batch cannabis from its farm south of Buffalo and small craft growers across Central and Western NY.

Hero CTAs:

- Primary: `Explore Farm to Flame`
- Secondary: `View Current Menu`
- Tertiary link or lower-priority CTA: `Plan Your Visit`

The hero should not over-prioritize shopping. It should make the visitor want to understand the place and the farm network first.

### 2. Local Farm Network

This is the main proof section. It should show that Garden of Weeden is connected to real regional producers.

Required content:

- Garden of Weeden's own farm south of Buffalo.
- Garden of Weeden name-brand flower and pre-rolls grown by the company selling them.
- No middleman for Garden of Weeden branded flower.
- Small craft growers in Central and Western NY.
- Small family farms.
- Women-owned, veteran-owned, minority-owned, and microbusiness partners.
- Explicit Cannabis Farmers Alliance mention.

The section should avoid sounding like a corporate supplier list. It should feel like a curated local network.

### 3. What Farm to Flame Means

This section should translate the brand idea into customer benefit.

Required points:

- Budtenders know the farmer partners.
- The team understands cultivation practices.
- Garden of Weeden branded flower and pre-rolls are grown by Garden of Weeden's own farm 15 miles south of Buffalo.
- The customer can buy a Garden of Weeden joint knowing it came from the same microbusiness they are buying it from.
- Products are sourced directly from local and regional growers.
- Customers get product stories and guidance, not anonymous shelf inventory.

This section can use process language such as farm, harvest, cure, selection, shelf, and flame.

### 4. Current Craft Menu

The menu section should be useful but secondary. It should support the All Leaves menu integration.

Required behavior:

- Provide a clear CTA to browse current availability.
- Explain that local releases and harvest availability can change.
- Avoid presenting the whole homepage as an ecommerce grid.

Primary CTA:

- `View Current Menu`

### 5. Forbidden Fruit Lounge And Private Events

This section should position the lounge as the place to experience the Farm to Flame concept socially.

Required content:

- On-site cannabis consumption lounge.
- Fun and educational cannabis events.
- Private event scheduling.
- Bookable mobile weed bar experience for parties and events, planned within New York cannabis rules.
- Contact Jennifer Fornell at `(716) 420-1591` for private event scheduling.

The section should feel inviting and premium, but still clear and practical.

### 6. On The Route

Cycling should be included as a subtle local stop signal.

Required framing:

- Garden of Weeden is an easy stop for people passing through the neighborhood or along nearby bike routes.
- Keep it minimal and local.
- Do not make the homepage feel bicycle-themed.

Possible copy direction:

> On the route, rooted in the neighborhood.

### 7. Community, Education, Events, Instagram

This section should push ongoing engagement.

Required content:

- Follow the events calendar in store or on Instagram.
- Garden of Weeden hosts fun and educational cannabis events.
- The brand wants to build ongoing customer relationships and a strong cannabis buying experience.

### 8. Footer And Compliance

The footer should remain compliance-forward and useful.

Required content:

- HOPEline information.
- Age/compliance framing.
- Phone number.
- Address and hours if available.
- Instagram.
- Menu link.
- Events/private events contact path.

## Visual Direction

Use a Local Farm First visual system with a warm dark garden feel:

- Dark, refined backgrounds.
- Warm farm imagery and cultivation textures.
- Clear product and grower story moments.
- Less fantasy/gaming styling.
- Less generic dispensary styling.
- Premium but local, not luxury-corporate.

The homepage should use real or real-feeling cannabis, farm, cultivation, lounge, and regional imagery. It should avoid decorative visual noise, repeated card grids, and overuse of glowing green effects.

## Navigation And CTA Hierarchy

Primary homepage CTA hierarchy:

1. Explore Farm to Flame.
2. Plan Your Visit or Events / Lounge.
3. View Current Menu.

Global nav should keep the menu accessible, but the homepage should not treat ecommerce as the primary brand promise.

## Accessibility And Compliance Requirements

- Keep the HOPEline banner visible and readable.
- Maintain age-gated/compliance-safe cannabis language.
- Avoid medical claims.
- Avoid implying guaranteed effects.
- Use accessible contrast for CTA buttons and text overlays.
- Use semantic sections and headings.
- Keep keyboard navigation intact.
- Ensure embedded menu fallback link is available if an iframe or script embed fails.

## Out Of Scope For This Spec

- Full redesign of About, Products, Shop, Cart, Checkout, Blog, or Admin pages.
- Replacing the All Leaves integration with a custom product API.
- Building a new event booking system.
- Changing legal or regulatory policy beyond homepage copy and layout.

## Success Criteria

- A first-time visitor understands Garden of Weeden as a local Farm to Flame cannabis microbusiness within the first viewport.
- The homepage clearly communicates the local farm network and explicitly mentions Cannabis Farmers Alliance.
- The lounge and private event path is visible and compelling without overtaking the farm-first story.
- The menu is easy to find but does not dominate the homepage.
- The cyclist signal is present as a subtle local route convenience.
- The page feels more cohesive, premium, regional, and conversion-oriented than the current homepage.
